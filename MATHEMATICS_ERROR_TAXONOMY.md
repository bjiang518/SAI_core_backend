# Mathematics Error Analysis Taxonomy

## Hierarchical Structure
**Level 1**: Mathematics (Subject)
**Level 2**: Base Branch (Major content domains/chapters)
**Level 3**: Detailed Branch (Specific topics/subtopics)

This taxonomy is based on Common Core State Standards for Mathematics (CCSSM), standard US K-12 mathematics curriculum, and typical textbook organizations.

---

## Base Branch 1: **Number & Operations**
**Grade Range**: K-8 (with foundational concepts continuing into high school)

### Detailed Branches:
1. **Whole Number Operations** (K-5)
   - Addition and subtraction
   - Multiplication and division
   - Order of operations
   - Place value understanding
   - Estimation and rounding

2. **Fraction Concepts & Operations** (3-7)
   - Understanding fractions as parts of a whole
   - Equivalent fractions
   - Adding and subtracting fractions
   - Multiplying and dividing fractions
   - Mixed numbers and improper fractions

3. **Decimal Operations** (4-7)
   - Place value with decimals
   - Adding and subtracting decimals
   - Multiplying decimals
   - Dividing decimals
   - Converting between fractions and decimals

4. **Integers & Rational Numbers** (6-8)
   - Positive and negative numbers
   - Number line representation
   - Integer operations (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division)
   - Absolute value
   - Rational number properties

5. **Ratios, Rates, & Proportions** (6-8)
   - Ratio concepts and notation
   - Unit rates
   - Proportional relationships
   - Solving proportions
   - Scale factors and similar figures

6. **Percent Concepts & Applications** (6-8)
   - Understanding percent
   - Percent of a quantity
   - Percent increase and decrease
   - Simple interest
   - Tax, tip, and discount problems

7. **Number Theory** (4-8)
   - Factors and multiples
   - Prime and composite numbers
   - Greatest common factor (GCF)
   - Least common multiple (LCM)
   - Divisibility rules

8. **Exponents & Powers** (6-9)
   - Positive integer exponents
   - Exponent rules and properties

   - Negative and zero exponents
   - Powers of ten

## Usage Notes for Error Analysis System

### Mapping Student Errors to Taxonomy:
1. **Identify the grade level** of the problem (helps narrow base branch)
2. **Determine the primary mathematical content** (base branch)
3. **Identify the specific skill/concept** being tested (detailed branch)
4. **Tag errors** with both base branch and detailed branch for precise tracking

### Example Error Tagging:
- **Student Error**: "Student incorrectly added fractions without finding common denominator: 1/2 + 1/3 = 2/5"
  - **Base Branch**: Number & Operations
  - **Detailed Branch**: Fraction Concepts & Operations
  - **Specific Issue**: Adding fractions with unlike denominators

- **Student Error**: "Student forgot to distribute negative sign: -(3x + 5) = -3x + 5"
  - **Base Branch**: Algebra - Foundations
  - **Detailed Branch**: Variables & Expressions
  - **Specific Issue**: Distributive property with negative

- **Student Error**: "Student confused sine and cosine: Used sin(30°) instead of cos(30°) to find adjacent side"
  - **Base Branch**: Trigonometry
  - **Detailed Branch**: Trigonometric Functions - Unit Circle (or Right Triangle Trigonometry)
  - **Specific Issue**: Trigonometric ratio selection

### Cross-Reference Features:
Some topics appear in multiple base branches (e.g., "Pythagorean Theorem" in both Geometry and Trigonometry). The system should:
- **Primary tag**: Where concept is first/primarily taught
- **Secondary tag**: Related advanced applications

### Grade-Level Guidance:
- **Elementary (K-5)**: Number & Operations, Basic Geometry
- **Middle School (6-8)**: Pre-Algebra, Ratios/Proportions, Statistics basics
- **High School (9-10)**: Algebra I-II, Geometry
- **Advanced High School (11-12)**: Trigonometry, Pre-Calculus, Calculus, Statistics
